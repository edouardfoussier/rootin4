## The pitch

**Rootin4** — *"Know who's really playing at your seat."*

Every other World Cup product predicts who wins the tournament. **Rootin4
predicts who actually shows up at the seat you already bought.** The 2026
FIFA World Cup runs across the United States, Canada, and Mexico from June
11 to July 19, with 48 teams and 104 fixtures across 16 stadiums. Most
ticket-holders bought their knockout-stage seats months before the bracket
was decided, so what they got on their ticket reads like a riddle —
*"Winner Group K vs third-place team from Group D/E/I/J/L"*. Rootin4 turns
that riddle into a probability per team and a probability per likely
pairing, refreshed continuously as news breaks.

The user is **Marco**, 32, from Lyon. He owns a Round-of-32 ticket and
doesn't know who'll be on the pitch. He isn't a stats nerd; he wants the
math translated into a calm, intelligent answer to three questions: *is my
team likely to play here, what are the realistic matchups, and is it worth
flying in?* Behind the curtain, an autonomous agent simulates the rest of
the tournament thousands of times, reads news to update its priors,
evaluates its own past predictions after every completed match, identifies
its own systematic biases, and corrects itself. The product is a wrapper
around a system that *learns* — and the visitor should feel that.

Design it however you like. Visual identity, color, typography, motion,
iconography, page architecture, component vocabulary — all of it is yours
to invent. Surprise me.
